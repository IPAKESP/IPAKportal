Training & Development Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines on the Training and Development of employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Introduction

For every development need, training is not the solution. To cater to employee learning needs according to their agility, there are three methods at IPAK:

  • Training Based Learning
  • Relationship Based Learning
  • Experiential Learning

2.2. Training Based Learning

Internal Trainings: Departments identify Subject Matter Experts (SME) to act as Internal Trainers.

External Trainings: Needs that cannot be addressed in-house. HR assesses these through Training Need Analysis (TNA).

International Trainings: Business-required training abroad, pre-approved by Management. Materials must be submitted to HR upon return.

2.3. Relationship Based Learning

Includes assigning mentors (SMEs), shadowing, and high-recommendation projects for those in Succession Planning. Includes Coaching and Mentorship Programs.

2.4. Experiential Learning

On-the-job training using short-term projects assigned by the Line Manager to master core skills.

2.5. Training Framework

Evolves around three pillars: Competence Framework, Capability Building, and Leadership & Management Skills.

2.6. Training Assessment (Kirk Patrick Model)

Training Feedback: Analyzes session quality (content, relevance, trainer).

Learning Assessment: Evaluates impact via Pre & Post quizzes. Scores below 70% require retraining.

2.7. Skill Matrix

A tool to identify skill gaps. Levels range from "No knowledge" to "Can train others." Visited twice a year by Line Managers.

2.8. Training Need Analysis (TNA)

HR develops TNA based on Competence Framework, Skill Matrix, and Leadership needs.

2.9. Training Calendar

HR finalizes a calendar based on TNA and shares it with departments.

2.10. Budget & Approvals

Managed within the annual approved training budget. Additional costs require Head of HR and CEO approval.

2.11. Process Flow

  1. HR initiates "Organizational Development Booklet."
  2. Employee raises request; Line Manager validates.
  3. Dept. Head finalizes; HR confirms enrollment.
  4. Employee participates; HR ensures assessment.
  5. HR furnishes assessment to Line Manager for further planning.
