IPAK Grading Structure

2.2. Grading Structure & Job Evaluation Process

  1. All the departments will follow the approved Grading Structure only.
  2. All the positions will be assessed through Job Evaluation and based on the results, the specific Grade will be assigned.
  3. To assess the position in the organization, HR Department will assess the position based on the following factors:
    • Impact: This factor will determine the size of the organization and the impact that a position has on the organization within the context of its contribution.
    • Communication: This factor will determine the nature of the position's communication responsibility, both within and outside the organization.
    • Innovation: This factor will determine the expected level of innovation from the position and the complexity of the innovation activity.
    • Knowledge: This factor relates to the level of knowledge required in the job to accomplish objectives and create value.
  4. All the positions in the organization will be assessed before adding in the organizational structure and the recommendations will be shared with the Management.

2.3. Career Track at IPAK

  1. To have a successful career at IPAK, important factors are sustained performance, education level and experience.
  2. The requirements to identify the grade level of a position for hiring and promotion will be based on the Career Mapping Sheet developed by HR Department.

2.4. Grading Structure & Position Titles

  1. Position title can be different, but the grade will remain the same.
  2. Through Job Evaluations, the recommendations will be provided to the Management on the placement of the position in the relevant grade.
  3. Line Manager will consult the HR Department for the addition of a position title in the organization.
  4. Benefits are linked to Grades and position titles cannot determine the Compensation & Benefits.

2.5. New Position & Grading

  1. In case of the requirement of any new position, the position will be evaluated.
  2. The evaluation will define the grade of the position in the organization.
  3. The grade will define the related compensation and benefits of the position.

Substance Abuse Testing Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

IPAK maintains a strict policy against the use of unlawful/prohibited drugs while at work or on company premises.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Policy Details

  1. To facilitate the administration and enforcement of this policy, IPAK may require or request employees to submit drug or substance abuse testing under certain circumstances.
  2. In cases where an employee's manager has reasonable suspicion to believe that the employee possesses or is under the influence of drugs, substance or drug screening may be ordered.
  3. HOD will inform HR Department for such related matters to proceed with the required actions according to the policy.
  4. This suspicion must be based on any objective symptoms, such as factors related to the employee's appearance, behavior, speech and/or other factors.
  5. If an employee is on medically prescribed medication, it is the employee's responsibility to advise his/her supervisor of this fact before he or she reports to work.
  6. Testing may also be required if an employee is found to be in possession of physical substances that could relate to the use of an illicit drug.
  7. Such evidence includes, but is not limited to drugs, drug paraphernalia or alcohol.
  8. Possession of drugs or alcohol on IPAK's property is prohibited whether or not it is determined that the employee also used such substances.

2.2. Grading Disciplinary Action

  1. The Management shall examine any such findings and decide which action should be taken against the accused.
  2. In case of any criminal breach, the company reserves the right to refer the matter to state authorities for an appropriate action in accordance with law.

Disciplinary Matters Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of the policy is to describe the standards of discipline/conduct to be observed by the employees and lays down the process to be followed to ensure a fair and transparent process of handling in-discipline/misconduct cases.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Discipline

Standards of conduct have been embodied in the:

  1. IPAK Code of Conduct
  2. Applicable laws or government regulations
  3. IPAK Policy Manuals & SOPs
  4. Any other policy of IPAK

2.2. Examples Of Misconduct

  1. Willful in-subordination or disobedience to any lawful and reasonable directions of a Line Manager or Management.
  2. Theft, Fraud, or dishonesty in connection IPAK's business or property.
  3. Taking or giving bribes or any illegal gratification.
  4. Riotous or disorderly behavior during working hours.
  5. Malingering/faking illness or other incapability to avoid duty or work.
  6. Harassment of other employees.
  7. Habitual absence without leave.
  8. Habitual late attendance.
  9. Habitual breach of any law applicable to the establishment.
  10. Habitual negligence or neglect of work.
Note: Cases of sexual Harassment will be dealt with as defined in the law, "The Protection against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act 2010".

2.3. Disciplinary Matters Procedure

  1. When a Line Manager/HR Manager receives report about the alleged misconduct, he/she shall examine the situation for authenticity of facts within 48 hours.
  2. If the allegation amounts to misconduct, then "Show Cause Notice" shall be issued by HR Department.
  3. The accused should be given 3 calendar days (72 hours) to submit his/her written explanation.
  4. The show cause shall be served personally upon the employee concerned.
  5. In case the explanation is not found satisfactory, formal inquiry into the matter would be initiated.
  6. Head of HR after consultation with the Management, would finalize whether such inquiry would be conducted "on job" or "suspend from services till facts are clear".
  7. The Inquiry Officer shall write down all the proceedings and get the signature of the accused.
  8. The inquiry officer shall, after giving due opportunity of hearing and cross examination, submit his/her findings/report to the Management.

2.4. Disciplinary Action

The Management shall examine the inquiry findings and decide which action should be taken:

  1. Warning / Reprimand
  2. Demotion to a lower position
  3. Stoppage of one-year increment
  4. Seizure/reduction of perks & benefits
  5. Dismissal from service

2.5. Criminal Breach

In case of any criminal breach, the company reserves the right to refer the matter to state authorities.

2.6. Resolution

The disciplinary action may be in writing and given to the accused. If the allegations are not proven, the matter will be dropped.

Email Usage Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines to ensure the proper use of IPAK's email system and make users aware of what IPAK deems as acceptable or unacceptable use.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Rules & Requirements

  1. Email is a business communication tool and users are obliged to use this tool in a responsible, effective, and lawful manner.
  2. The following rules must be strictly adhered to. It is prohibited to:
    • Send or forward emails containing libelous, defamatory, offensive, racist or obscene remarks.
    • Forward or copy a message containing confidential or copyrighted information without prior approval.
    • Send unsolicited email messages or forge email messages.
    • Send email messages using another person's email account.
    • Send an attachment that contains a virus, spyware, adware, or any such malicious code.
  3. If you receive an email which is in violation of the above rules, you must promptly notify your Line Manager.

2.2. Writing Emails

  1. Write well-structured emails and use short, descriptive subjects.
  2. IPAK email style is informal. Signatures must include your name, job title and company name.
  3. Spell check all mails prior to transmission.
  4. Do not send unnecessary attachments. Compress attachments larger than 200K before sending.
  5. Do not write emails in capitals.
  6. Only mark emails as important if they really are important.

2.3. Personal Use

The company's email system is meant for business use only. All messages distributed via the company's email system are IPAK's property.

2.4. System Monitoring

Users of the company's email system expressly waive any right of privacy. IPAK may monitor emails without prior notification.

2.5. Declaration

Employees are required to sign a copy of the Email Policy declaring that they have read, understood, and will comply with the guidelines.

Gratuity Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide clarity on the gratuity of employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

No exception out of applicable laws will be admissible.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Introduction

  1. Gratuity is a terminal benefit at IPAK — a lump-sum amount payable to an employee on leaving service based on gross salary and period of service.
  2. It is a reward of good and efficient service for a substantial period of time.

2.2. Eligibility

  1. All the permanent employees will be entitled for the Gratuity.
  2. Temporary, badli, probationer or contract workers will not be eligible for gratuity.
  3. If an employee has completed six months or more of service, they will be entitled to gratuity for that full year.

2.3. Not Entitled for Payment of Gratuity

If an employee's services were terminated on account of misconduct (like harassment, theft etc.), gratuity would no longer be admissible.

2.4. Calculation of Gratuity

  1. The basis of gratuity calculation is the "Gross Salary" which includes Basic Salary, House Rent & Utilities.
  2. Any length of service higher than six months over the number of years is considered as one year for gratuity calculation.
  3. Any length of service less than six months is not included in calculating gratuity.
