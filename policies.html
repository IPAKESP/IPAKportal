Disciplinary Matters Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of the policy is to describe the standards of discipline/conduct to be observed by the employees and in case of in-discipline/misconduct, lays down the process to be followed to ensure a fair and transparent process of handling in-discipline /misconduct cases.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Discipline

Employee discipline or standard conduct means those standards which employee is expected to observe. Standards of conduct have been embodied in the:

  1. IPAK Code of Conduct
  2. Applicable laws or government regulations
  3. IPAK Policy Manuals & SOPs
  4. Any other policy of IPAK

Violation of these standards shall be treated as misconduct.

2.2. Examples Of Misconduct

  1. Willful in-subordination or disobedience, whether alone or in combination with others, to any lawful and reasonable directions of a Line Manager or Management.
  2. Theft, Fraud, or dishonesty in connection IPAK’s business or property.
  3. Taking or giving bribes or any illegal gratification.
  4. Riotous or disorderly behavior during working hours at the establishment or ay act subversive of discipline.
  5. Malingering/faking illness or other incapability to avoid duty or work.
  6. Harassment of other employees.
  7. Habitual absence without leave.
  8. Habitual late attendance.
  9. Habitual breach of any law applicable to the establishment.
  10. Habitual negligence or neglect of work.
Note: Cases of sexual Harassment will be dealt with as defined in the law, “The Protection against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act 2010”. The inquiry Committee constituted by the Company shall follow the process as defined in the law.

2.3. Disciplinary Matters Procedure

  1. When a Line Manager/ HR Manager receives report about the alleged misconduct on the part of an employee, he/she shall examine the situation for authenticity of facts within 48 hours of such report and determine whether the allegations amount to misconduct. Line Manager may take advice from HR Department.
  2. If the allegation amounts to misconduct, then “Show Cause Notice” shall be issued by HR Department.
  3. The accused should be given 3 calendar days (72 hours) to submit his/her written explanation.
  4. The show cause shall be served personally upon the employee concerned. In case the employee is absent, it shall be sent to him by post on the last available address.
  5. In case, the employee is present but avoids receiving the show cause, the statement of the person who was deputed to serve the show cause and of two other witnesses shall be recorded in writing and thereafter the Show Cause shall be sent to him at the last available address by post.
  6. On receipt of the written explanation by the accused employee, it shall be examined to see whether the explanation is satisfactory or not. If the explanation is found to be satisfactory, the matter will be dropped, and the accused shall be informed accordingly. If it is such that it does not call for any action except advice, the accused shall be issued Advisory letter.
  7. In case the explanation is not found satisfactory, or the accused fails to submit his explanation, then formal inquiry into the matter would be initiated.
  8. Head of HR after consultation with the Management, would finalize either such inquiry would be conducted “on job” or “to suspend from services till facts are clear”.
  9. Any person in the management cadre, employed at any location of the company, who is neither witness to the occurrence of the act of misconduct in question nor is concerned with charge in question, can be appointed as an Inquiry Officer by Head of HR after consultation with the Management.
  10. The Inquiry Officer shall write down all the proceedings in English or Urdu and get the signature of the accused.
  11. The inquiry officer shall, after giving due opportunity of hearing and cross examination, submit his/her findings/report to the Management.

2.4. Disciplinary Action

The Management shall examine the inquiry findings and decide which of the following action should be taken against the accused:

  1. Warning / Reprimand
  2. Demotion to a lower position
  3. Stoppage of one-year increment
  4. Seizure/reduction of perks & benefits
  5. Dismissal from service

2.5. Criminal Breach

In case of any criminal breach, the company reserves the right to refer the matter to state authorities for an appropriate action in accordance with law.

2.6. Resolution

The disciplinary action may be in writing and given to the accused. If the allegations are not proven, the matter will be dropped, and the accused shall be informed accordingly.

Dress Code Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines on the Dress Code for employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Dress Code

Office based Employees & Field Staff

  1. All employees are expected to always use their own good judgment and dress appropriately to project a professional image
  2. Business formal attire which includes dress shirt & trousers is expected at a minimum in general on all weekdays (Monday to Thursday).
  3. Business formal which includes dress shirt with necktie and suit for internal important meetings and meetings with external parties and customers where required.
  4. Fridays shall be observed as semi-casual days except in case of important meetings are scheduled. An employee can wear appropriate Shalwar Kameez with Waistcoat/Coat.
  5. Saturdays shall be observed as casual days except in case of important meetings are scheduled. An employee can wear appropriate Jeans or Chinos with color T-Shirt/Polo Shirt.
  6. Appropriate closed formal shoes are allowed in the office.
  7. The employees can dress up in wearing Shalwar Kameez only with Formal Coat/Waist Coat and with formal shoes/closed shoes/Peshawari Khairi
  8. All employees are expected to wear decent office colors
  9. Jewelry, makeup, perfume, and cologne should be in good taste
  10. For employees and teams involved in operations requiring customer or Govt. official meetings or other forms of formal meetings, it is expected to formal business attire which can include a buttoned-down shirt and trousers / suite with necktie.
  11. Slippers to be used only during prayer times. It is not expected from employees that they use slippers in the office after prayers.

Plant based Employees

  1. All employees will ensure to always use the uniform in the plant premises
  2. Only the prescribed uniform by the company will be acceptable which includes the complete kit (shirt, trousers, and safety shoes)
  3. No Jewelry item is allowed in the plant area to avoid any safety risk
  4. During Prayer Breaks, slippers to be used only around designated prayer area. It is not expected from employees that they use slippers inside the plant area.

2.2. Visitors at Plant

  1. No visitor will be allowed in the plant area without safety briefing and will be provided with a Visitor Safety Jacket (Visitor will be written on the jacket clearly)
  2. This is the responsibility of the host to ensure that all the requirements are met
  3. All such noncompliance will be reported to the Safety Steering Committee

2.3. Employees Visiting at Plant

  1. All the employee visiting plant either from any other location of the company or from offices in the same premises, will ensure that they wear safety shoes (In case of visit to process area) and safety jacket before they enter the plant area where protocol defines as per Safety Policy.
  2. If there is any construction or repair work is in process and Safety Team has identified the area as safety hazard, employee will go to such area with safety helmet.

2.4. Third Party Compliance

  1. All the third-party contractors will ensure that their employees wear their uniforms before entering the premises
  2. Safety department may stop the work if the compliance to uniform is not ensured which also includes safety shoes and helmets
  3. If any third-party employee doesn’t have specific uniform, a safety jacket is mandatory and contractor name to be written
  4. Procurement Team will ensure that before assigning any work to the third-party contractor, these terms are agreed and complied
  5. Security will ensure compliance before allowing access

2.5. Personal Attire & Hygiene

  1. All employees are expected to ensure good personal hygiene
  2. To ensure a presentable appearance, all employees are required to shave daily or in case of beard, it should be trimmed properly
  3. Appropriate hair style is expected from employees to give a decent & professional look
  4. A cologne or body spray may be used to avoid inconvenience to other colleagues due to body odor

2.6. What is not allowed

Following items will not be appreciated in the office environment:

  1. flip-flops or Hawaii slippers
  2. flashy athletic shoes
  3. men’s open-toe shoes and light walks
  4. shorts
  5. Bermuda
  6. men’s sleeveless shirts
  7. sweatpants & sweatshirts
  8. exercise pants
  9. overalls
  10. leggings
  11. muscle tops
  12. shirts with potentially offensive words, terms, logo, pictures, or slogans
  13. Outrageous, provocative, or inappropriate attire is strictly not allowed
  14. Torn, dirty, or frayed clothing

2.7. Compliance

  1. This is the responsibility of the Line Managers to ensure that the employees ensure compliance to the policy.
  2. Repeated noncompliance will be reported to HR Department
