2.2. Grading Structure & Job Evaluation Process

  1. All the departments will follow the approved Grading Structure only.
  2. All the positions will be assessed through Job Evaluation and based on the results, the specific Grade will be assigned.
  3. To assess the position in the organization, HR Department will assess the position based on the following factors:
    • Impact: This factor will determine the size of the organization and the impact that a position has on the organization within the context of its contribution.
    • Communication: This factor will determine the nature of the position's communication responsibility, both within and outside the organization.
    • Innovation: This factor will determine the expected level of innovation from the position and the complexity of the innovation activity. This factor relates to the requirements of the position to identify and make improvement to procedures, services, or products as well as to develop new ideas, methods, techniques, services, and products.
    • Knowledge: This factor relates to the level of knowledge required in the job to accomplish objectives and create value and the application of knowledge in teams and in groups and cultures and environments the position operates.
  4. All the positions in the organization will be assessed before adding in the organizational structure and the recommendations will be shared with the Management.

2.3. Career Track at IPAK

  1. To have a successful career at IPAK, important factors are sustained performance, education level and experience. The year of service and education will not define the success of the employee only, but sustained performance will define the growth based on available positions in the organization.
  2. The requirements to identify the grade level of a position for hiring and promotion will be based on the Career Mapping Sheet developed by HR Department based on internal and external equity.

2.4. Grading Structure & Position Titles

  1. Position title can be different, but the grade will remain the same.
  2. Through Job Evaluations, the recommendations will be provided to the Management on the placement of the position in the relevant grade.
  3. Line Manager will consult the HR Department for the addition of a position title in the organization.
  4. Benefits are linked to Grades and position titles cannot determine the Compensation & Benefits.

2.5. New Position & Grading

  1. In case of the requirement of any new position, the position will be evaluated.
  2. The evaluation will define the grade of the position in the organization.
  3. The grade will define the related compensation and benefits of the position.

Substance Abuse Testing Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

IPAK maintains a strict policy against the use of unlawful/prohibited drugs while at work or on company premises. The objective of this policy is to define the complete guidelines on the substance abuse testing for the guidelines of the Employees and Line Managers.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Policy Details

  1. To facilitate the administration and enforcement of this policy, IPAK may require or request employees to submit drug or substance abuse testing under certain circumstances.
  2. In cases where an employee's manager has reasonable suspicion to believe that the employee possesses or is under the influence of drugs and/or related substances and such use or influence may adversely affect the employee's job performance, or the safety of the employee or co-workers, substance or drug screening may be ordered.
  3. HOD will inform HR Department for such related matters to proceed with the required actions according to the policy.
  4. This suspicion must be based on any objective symptoms, such as factors related to the employee's appearance, behavior, speech and/or other factors.
  5. If an employee is on medically prescribed medication, it is the employee's responsibility to advise his/her supervisor of this fact before he or she reports to work.
  6. Testing may also be required if an employee is found to be in possession of physical substances — either on their person or at their workstation or locker — that could relate to the use of an illicit drug.
  7. Such evidence includes, but is not limited to drugs, drug paraphernalia or alcohol.
  8. It should be emphasized that possession of drugs or alcohol on IPAK's property is prohibited whether or not it is determined that the employee also used such substances.

2.2. Grading Disciplinary Action

  1. The Management shall examine any such findings and decide which action should be taken against the accused.
  2. In case of any criminal breach, the company reserves the right to refer the matter to state authorities for an appropriate action in accordance with law.

Disciplinary Matters Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of the policy is to describe the standards of discipline/conduct to be observed by the employees and in case of in-discipline/misconduct, lays down the process to be followed to ensure a fair and transparent process of handling in-discipline /misconduct cases.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Discipline

Employee discipline or standard conduct means those standards which employee is expected to observe. Standards of conduct have been embodied in the:

  1. IPAK Code of Conduct
  2. Applicable laws or government regulations
  3. IPAK Policy Manuals & SOPs
  4. Any other policy of IPAK

Violation of these standards shall be treated as misconduct.

2.2. Examples Of Misconduct

  1. Willful in-subordination or disobedience, whether alone or in combination with others, to any lawful and reasonable directions of a Line Manager or Management.
  2. Theft, Fraud, or dishonesty in connection IPAK's business or property.
  3. Taking or giving bribes or any illegal gratification.
  4. Riotous or disorderly behavior during working hours at the establishment or ay act subversive of discipline.
  5. Malingering/faking illness or other incapability to avoid duty or work.
  6. Harassment of other employees.
  7. Habitual absence without leave.
  8. Habitual late attendance.
  9. Habitual breach of any law applicable to the establishment.
  10. Habitual negligence or neglect of work.
Note: Cases of sexual Harassment will be dealt with as defined in the law, "The Protection against Harassment of Women at the Workplace Act 2010". The inquiry Committee constituted by the Company shall follow the process as defined in the law.

2.3. Disciplinary Matters Procedure

  1. When a Line Manager/ HR Manager receives report about the alleged misconduct on the part of an employee, he/she shall examine the situation for authenticity of facts within 48 hours of such report and determine whether the allegations amount to misconduct. Line Manager may take advice from HR Department.
  2. If the allegation amounts to misconduct, then "Show Cause Notice" shall be issued by HR Department.
  3. The accused should be given 3 calendar days (72 hours) to submit his/her written explanation.
  4. The show cause shall be served personally upon the employee concerned. In case the employee is absent, it shall be sent to him by post on the last available address.
  5. In case, the employee is present but avoids receiving the show cause, the statement of the person who was deputed to serve the show cause and of two other witnesses shall be recorded in writing and thereafter the Show Cause shall be sent to him at the last available address by post.
  6. On receipt of the written explanation by the accused employee, it shall be examined to see whether the explanation is satisfactory or not. If the explanation is found to be satisfactory, the matter will be dropped, and the accused shall be informed accordingly. If it is such that it does not call for any action except advice, the accused shall be issued Advisory letter.
  7. In case the explanation is not found satisfactory, or the accused fails to submit his explanation, then formal inquiry into the matter would be initiated.
  8. Head of HR after consultation with the Management, would finalize either such inquiry would be conducted "on job" or "to suspend from services till facts are clear".
  9. Any person in the management cadre, employed at any location of the company, who is neither witness to the occurrence of the act of misconduct in question nor is concerned with charge in question, can be appointed as an Inquiry Officer by Head of HR after consultation with the Management.
  10. The Inquiry Officer shall write down all the proceedings in English or Urdu and get the signature of the accused.
  11. The inquiry officer shall, after giving due opportunity of hearing and cross examination, submit his/her findings/report to the Management.

2.4. Disciplinary Action

The Management shall examine the inquiry findings and decide which of the following action should be taken against the accused:

  1. Warning / Reprimand
  2. Demotion to a lower position
  3. Stoppage of one-year increment
  4. Seizure/reduction of perks & benefits
  5. Dismissal from service

2.5. Criminal Breach

In case of any criminal breach, the company reserves the right to refer the matter to state authorities for an appropriate action in accordance with law.

2.6. Resolution

The disciplinary action may be in writing and given to the accused. If the allegations are not proven, the matter will be dropped, and the accused shall be informed accordingly.

Email Usage Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines to ensure the proper use of IPAK's email system and make users aware of what International Packaging Ltd. deems as acceptable or unacceptable use of its email system.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Rules & Requirements

  1. Email is a business communication tool and users are obliged to use this tool in a responsible, effective, and lawful manner.
  2. Although by its nature email seems to be less formal than other written communication, however, the same laws apply.
  3. Therefore, it is important that users are aware of the rules, requirements, and risks of email.
  4. Please note that you may be held liable for legal as well as disciplinary action if the rules in this Email Policy are violated in any way.
  5. The following rules must be strictly adhered to. It is prohibited to:
    • Send or forward emails containing libelous, defamatory, offensive, racist or obscene remarks or emails containing religious material/comments.
    • Forward or copy a message containing confidential or copyrighted information without acquiring prior approval from the competent authorities.
    • Send unsolicited email messages.
    • Forge or attempt to forge email messages.
    • Disguise or attempt to disguise your identity when sending mail.
    • Send email messages using another person's email account.
    • Send an attachment that contains a virus, spyware, adware, or any such malicious code.
  6. If you receive an email which is in violation of the above rules, you must promptly notify your Line Manager.

2.2. Best Practices

IPAK considers email as an important means of communication and recognizes the importance of proper email content and speedy replies in conveying a professional image and delivering good customer service. Users should take the same care in drafting an email as they would for any other communication.

2.3. Writing Emails

  1. Write well-structured emails and use short, descriptive subjects.
  2. When addressing clients make sure that you are authorized to write directly to customer representatives – if in doubt check with your Line Manager.
  3. When including customer representatives in a list of recipients, take extra care to ensure that the style and content of the mail is appropriate.
  4. IPAK email style is informal. This means that sentences can be short and to the point. You may start your email simply with the name of the person by adding Mr. / Ms. before the name. Messages may be ended with 'Regards'. The use of Internet abbreviations, smileys, and religious greetings are discouraged.
  5. Signatures must include your name, job title and company name. A disclaimer may be added underneath your signature but that should only be the standard and approved by the Management.
  6. Spell check all mails prior to transmission.
  7. Do not send unnecessary attachments. It is recommended to compress attachments larger than 200K before sending them.
  8. Do not write emails in capitals.
  9. Do not use cc: or bcc: fields unless absolutely required and the recipient is aware what action, if any, to take.
  10. If you forward mails, state clearly what action you expect the recipient to take.
  11. Only send emails of which the content could be displayed on a public notice board. If they cannot be displayed publicly in their current state, consider rephrasing the email, using other means of communication, or protecting information by using a password.
  12. Only mark emails as important if they really are important.

2.4. Maintenance

Delete any email messages that you do not need to have a copy of and set your email client to automatically empty your 'deleted items' on closing.

2.5. Personal Use

The company's email system is meant for business use only and emails for personal use are not allowed. IPAK discourages the sending of chain letters, junk mail, jokes, and executables. All messages distributed via the company's email system are IPAK's property.

2.6. Email Quota

All users are provided a specific mailbox space on the exchange server. All users are recommended to keep checking their mailbox space utilization at regular intervals and must maintain their mailbox size at a level to avoid an 'out of storage space' situation.

2.7. Email Accounts

All email accounts maintained on our email systems are the property of IPAK. Passwords should not be given to other people and should be changed as per direction of the IT Department.

2.8. System Monitoring

Users of the company's email system expressly waive any right of privacy in anything they create, store, send or receive on the company's computer system. IPAK may monitor emails without prior notification. If there is evidence that anyone is not adhering to the guidelines set out in this policy, IPAK reserves the right to take disciplinary action.

2.9. Disclaimer

The following disclaimer may be added to each outgoing email:

'This electronic mail is intended only for the addressee(s). Any use, distribution, copying or disclosure by unauthorized personnel is strictly prohibited'.

2.10. Declaration

Employees are required to sign a copy of the Email Policy declaring that they have read, understood, and acknowledged receipt of the Email policy, and that they will comply with the guidelines set out in this policy and have understood.

Electronic Communication Usage Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

IPAK has established the Internet, Intranet, e-mail, and electronic communication policy to make certain that employees utilize electronic communications devices in a legal, ethical, and appropriate manner. The objective of this policy is to address the company's legal responsibilities and concerns regarding the fair and proper use of all electronic communications devices within the company.

1.2. Scope

This policy extends to all features of the company's electronic communications systems, including all computing equipment (hardware and software), networking equipment, connections to the Internet, and all voice and video equipment, i.e., any form of electronic communication used by employees currently or in the future.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Standards and Rules

  1. It may not be possible to identify every standard and rule applicable to the use of electronic communications devices. Employees are therefore encouraged to utilize sound judgment whenever using any feature of the communications systems.
  2. To offer employees some guidance, the following principles and standards should be clearly understood and followed:
    • The company's policy against harassment, including sexual harassment, extends to the use of computers, the Internet, and any component of the communications systems. In keeping with that policy, employees should not use any electronic communications device in a manner that would violate that policy.
    • The company's anti-discrimination policies extend to the use of communications systems. Any employee who uses any electronic communications device in any manner that violates the company's anti-discrimination policies or commitment to equal employment opportunity will therefore be subject to disciplinary action, including the possibility of immediate termination.
    • Employees may not use an electronic communications device for a purpose that is found to constitute, in the company's sole and absolute discretion, a commercial use that is not for the direct and immediate benefit of the company or that is competitive to the interests of the company.
    • Employees may not use any electronic communications device in a manner that violates the trademark, copyright, or license rights of any other person, entity or organization.
    • Employees should identify all communications as "privileged and confidential" when it is accurate and appropriate to do so. In this manner, the company can assert any protections, privileges and rights relating to communications if it becomes necessary to do so.

2.2. Access

  1. IPAK must retain the right and ability to enforce policy and to monitor compliance with its terms. While computers and other electronic devices are made accessible to employees to assist them in performing their jobs and to promoting the company's interests, all computers and electronic devices must remain fully accessible to the company, and will remain the property of IPAK.
  2. While IPAK respects the privacy of employee's working environment, it retains the right to gain access to any information received by, transmitted by, or stored in any such electronic communications device, either with or without an employee's knowledge, consent, or approval.

2.3. Telephone Monitoring

  1. Company telephone lines are limited and are designed only for business use.
  2. Personal phone calls should be kept to a minimum justified level during working hours.
  3. If required, the company may also monitor the usage of telephone systems by employees.

2.4. Cell Phone Usage

  1. While the nature of some employees' work necessitates that those employees may not take their cell phones to their workplace, the company generally allows employees to use their cell phones on company premises; however, employees are expected to exercise prudent judgment in keeping personal calls to a minimum.
  2. The following are some guidelines for proper cell phone usage at the office:
    • Loud and/or fancy ring tones can be offensive and inappropriate in the workplace.
    • Employees are advised to keep ring tones at minimum volume or to turn the ring tones low, while in the office area.
    • Employees should keep in mind that cell phone conversations can be loud, even when the parties think that they are being quiet.
    • Camera phones must not be used in or around the workplace to capture images, without the prior consent of the person(s) in such images.
    • In-person conversations should not be interrupted for cell phone calls; however, if it is an urgent call, politely excuse yourself from the conversation before answering.
    • The company will not be liable for the loss of personal cell phones brought into the workplace.

2.5. Compliance is Essential

  1. Employees who violate any aspect of this policy or who demonstrate poor judgment in the way they use electronic communications devices will be subject to disciplinary action.
  2. Employees who have any questions regarding this policy should bring them to the immediate attention of the Human Resources Department.

Laptop Security Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to define the policy for security of a company provided laptop computer that includes the device as well as the business systems and data residing upon it. Further to clarify that the asset belongs to the organization and is assigned to the employee for business use only. This policy defines the employee's responsibility to use the assigned laptop properly, protect it from being damaged, stolen or misused and keep it in his/her safe custody.

1.2. Scope

This policy applies to all permanent and contractual employees of IPAK who have company provided laptops.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Laptop Security

  1. A laptop is assigned to the employee based on the role and nature of assigned work. The decision for the requirement of laptop will be made by the Management in accordance with the policy.
  2. Before assigning the laptop, Department Head shall discuss the prescribed data security controls with the employees, which are essential to keep the data safe.
  3. IT controls must be applied on each laptop. Other than this, more controls can also be applied (if required).
  4. IT Department will ensure the data security controls before handing over the laptop to the employee.
  5. It is the employee's responsibility to use the laptop properly, protect it from being damaged, stolen or misused and keep it in his/her safe custody.
  6. Employee shall use all possible means to keep this asset from being stolen.
  7. In case the laptop is stolen or damaged then the employee shall immediately inform, in writing his/her Department Head and provide complete incident details and/or legal proof of the laptop have been stolen.
  8. In case of theft, if employee negligence is proven then the employee shall contribute the cost of the replacement laptop price. The percentage of the cost will be decided by the Management.
  9. In case of damages, a diagnostic review will be carried out by the IT department and if damages are found to be caused by employee negligence, the employee shall pay the repair cost. All such recommendations will be presented to the Management for the final decision.
  10. The employee shall not leave the laptop unattended without proper locking (screen lock).
  11. The employee is accountable for any action taken by his/her user ID. Therefore, employee will not share username and password. This includes family and other household members when work is being done from home.
  12. IT Department will ensure that only that software is installed which are required to perform the assigned job only.
  13. The employee shall not alter the security and anti-virus configurations. In case of any issue, the employee shall establish contact with the IT support team.
  14. The employee shall take a backup of business-critical information on allocated space on the network to avoid any business loss in case the laptop is stolen.
  15. It shall be the employee's responsibility to protect all information stored on the laptop. As the laptop is given for business use only so the organization has the right to review data stored on the assigned laptop.
  16. The employee must ensure that the assigned laptop machine shall not be used for any kind of unlawful activity for example, software piracy, fraudulent activity, exporting software information, technical information, application code or technology, and other business-related information to unauthorized personnel.

2.2. Enforcement

Any employee found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action.

Gratuity Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide clarity on the gratuity of employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

No exception out of applicable laws will be admissible.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Introduction

  1. Gratuity is one of the prevalent retirement benefits for employees. Gratuity is actually a benefit for services rendered in the past. It is a reward of good, efficient and honest service for a substantial period of time.
  2. Gratuity is a terminal benefit at IPAK. It is a "lump-sum" amount of money payable to an employee on leaving service (through retirement, death or termination of service) based on salary (last drawn gross salary) and period of service (over and above six months).
  3. Gratuity is actually a benefit for services rendered in the past. It is a reward of good and efficient service for a substantial period of time.

2.2. Eligibility

  1. All the permanent employees will be entitled for the Gratuity.
  2. He/she must be a permanent employee. If an employee is temporary, badli, probationer or a contract worker, he/she will not be eligible for gratuity.
  3. Long term contract employees can be eligible for Gratuity subject to approval of the Management.
  4. If an employee has completed six months or more of service, they will be entitled to gratuity for that full year.

2.3. Entitled for Payment of Gratuity

  1. The employee who resigns from his service.
  2. The organization terminates his services due to reasons other than misconduct.
  3. The employee dies while in service.
  4. The employee reaches the superannuation age and retires.

2.4. Not Entitled for Payment of Gratuity

If an employee's services were terminated on account of misconduct (like harassment, theft etc.), gratuity would no longer be admissible to him.

2.5. Payment of Gratuity in Case of Death

  1. In case of death of an eligible employee, gratuity is payable to the legal dependents of the employee. Death may not necessarily occur on duty but the employee should have completed minimum 6 months on the job at the time of death.
  2. The payment to the dependents will be subject to the Succession Certificate issued by the competent court.

2.6. Calculation of Gratuity

  1. The basis for calculation of wages is "wages admissible to and in the last month of his/her service".
  2. The basis of gratuity calculation is the "Gross Salary" which includes Basic Salary, House Rent & Utilities and this is in line with the Remuneration policy of IPAK.
  3. Contingent or unpredictable payments like temporary relief to the employees if any or bonus etc. provided by IPAK will not be included in the gratuity calculation.
  4. Other than salary, the second determining factor in gratuity calculation is the period an employee has served with an establishment/organization.
  5. Any length of service higher than six months over the number of years of service is considered as one year for the purpose of gratuity calculation.
  6. Any length of service less than six months is not included in the course of calculating gratuity.

Hajj Balloting Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines on Hajj balloting for the employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the eligible employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the CEO.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Introduction

  1. The International Packaging Films Ltd. will sponsor Hajj for two (2) permanent employees through balloting every year.
  2. Employees who have completed their 05 years of service will be eligible for the Hajj Balloting.
  3. Employees from JM9 to MM6 will be eligible for balloting.
  4. Employees who have performed Hajj earlier under this policy will not be eligible for the balloting.
  5. To be eligible for the balloting, the duration of the employment with the organization should be without any gap or intervals.
  6. Employees with reported misconducts or inquiries will be subject to the approval of the management either to add such employees in the balloting or not.
  7. Hajj Balloting will be arranged based upon Government announcement for Hajj registration.
  8. If the employee refuses to proceed for Hajj, then the balloting will be conducted again, and the new employee will be announced.
  9. The amount of Hajj Package cannot be claimed in cash in any case.
  10. The results of the balloting cannot be transferred in the name of another employee.

2.2. Hajj Package

  1. Hajj booking will be processed under Government Scheme only.
  2. The Management may decide to change the package to private.
  3. If an employee wants to perform Hajj on private arrangements, the company will only pay the amount prescribed by Government Scheme and the differential will be paid upfront by the employee.
  4. If any employee wants to perform Hajj on private arrangement, such arrangements will be arranged by the Commercial Department and the employee will select the suitable option.
  5. If the name of the employee is not drawn in the Government policy, the employee will submit the application next year.
  6. The package will include Qurbani and other related expenses.

2.3. Hajj Leaves

Hajj Leaves will be managed through the applicable Leave Policy.

2.4. Financial Assistance

  1. IPAK will provide all necessary assistance to the employee for obtaining Visa.
  2. Fee and processing charges for Visa and passport will be paid by the company.
  3. The employee will ensure that his/her all documents are completed in all respects to facilitate early issue of Visa.
  4. Other than ticket arrangement, company will pay one basic salary for the personal expenses of the employee to support the employee in expenses related to his/her travel.

2.5. Travelling Guidelines

  1. As a general principle, travelling expenses paid to an employee is intended to cover his/her actual expenses and should not be a source of income.
  2. All travel requirements will be completed by the traveler prior to the application submission for Hajj to Administration Department.
  3. Air tickets will be arranged through company appointed/approved travel agencies or as prescribed by Government Authorities or Tour Agents. No advance will be allowed if any amount is outstanding in respect of previous travelling.
  4. Travelling will be admissible based on journey by the shortest route, that is to say, the route by which an employee can reach his/her destination in the shortest possible time by approved mode of travel.
  5. The employee will ensure that he/she is in possession of a valid passport, and if not, then to obtain the passport well in time.
  6. The Company will not pay for any personal baggage which exceeds the limits provided by the carrier.
  7. The Company will not be responsible if any contraband item is found in the personal baggage being carried by the employee. The employee will personally ensure that his/her personal baggage and the company baggage handed over to him/her is packed in his/her presence and does not contain any contraband item.
  8. Temporary health insurance shall be arranged by the Company for the period of stay abroad.
  9. Hajj arrangements will be managed by Administration Department after authorization by competent/controlling authority.

Training & Development Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

The objective of this policy is to provide guidelines on the Training and Development of employees at IPAK.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Introduction

This is important to provide the right source of learning to the employee according to his/her learning agility. For every development need, training is not the solution. So to cater the employee learning needs according to his/her learning agility, there are three methods at IPAK for the development of the employee:

  • Training Based Learning
  • Relationship Based Learning
  • Experiential Learning

2.2. Training Based Learning

Training is an important source of employee's development. There can be two sources of employee's training:

  • Internal Training
  • External Training
  • International Training

Internal Trainings

This is important to have internal resources to cater the basic development needs of the employee. The departments will identify the Subject Matter Experts (SME) for certain skills. These Subject Matter Experts will act as Internal Trainers who will facilitate the process by training employees internally on the skills related to their job roles.

External Trainings

For all such development needs which cannot be addressed in house, will be managed externally. Human Resource Department will assess the training needs which cannot be addressed internally. On the basis of such Training Need Analysis, following arrangements can be made:

  1. Employees can be sent to some external programs related to certain skills.
  2. External trainer can be engaged to deliver training in house on specific subject matter.
  3. On completion of an external training, the employee will submit a copy of the training material to Human Resource Department.

International Trainings

  1. In case of any business requirements, the employee can be sent abroad for training. All such trainings will be pre-approved by the Management.
  2. On completion of an international training, the employee will submit a copy of the training material to Human Resource Department.
  3. Human Resource Department will archive all such records for future reference and study.

2.3. Relationship Based Learning

  1. In some cases, employee can be assigned to a relationship-based learning where he/she can be assigned a mentor who is the Subject Matter Expert. The employee will shadow the Mentor to understand the process and learn the skill set.
  2. An employee can be assigned a project where he/she can interact with other teams and learn the processes. Such projects are highly recommended for those employees who are part of Succession Planning, and the management sees them in bigger roles in future.
  3. Line Manager will decide to engage the employee in any of such Relationship Based Learning.
  4. The employee can be engaged in Coaching and Mentorship Program as well.

2.4. Experiential Learning

Experiential Learning is an on job training which can be applied to the employee who have newly joined the organization or is working in the same capacity for some time. Line Manager will assign short term projects within his/her job role to strengthen the skill set. This will help the employee to focus on one skill at a time to master the core skill required in his/her job.

2.5. Training Framework

At IPAK, Training Framework is the base of employee development and his/her career growth. This is expected of every employee to live by the Framework to achieve organization's vision and personal development. Training Framework evolves around the following pillars:

  • Competence Framework
  • Capability Building
  • Leadership & Management Skills

Competence Framework

  1. The Competence Framework defines the competencies and key behaviors that embody our corporate values and form the basis of our growth and the development of the employees.
  2. Our prime focus is to ensure that all the employees are well versed with the Values, Vision & Mission, Core Competencies, and behaviors related to the Core Competencies.
  3. Human Resource Department will initiate the trainings for the employees to enhance the understanding and development of the employees.

Capability Building

  1. This is important to enhance the capability to perform the job of an employee. IPAK provides the way forward for the employees to understand their job and to fill gaps in accordance to Skill Matrix.
  2. These skills comprise of two elements:
    • Capabilities required to perform according to the Job Description of the employee
    • Day to Day job knowledge
  3. This is important to provide opportunities of learning to the employee to master the competencies to perform his/her job. These competencies are related to the Core job of the employee.

Leadership & Management Development

  1. This is important for any organization to develop its current and future leadership and IPAK encourages its employees to develop themselves to take senior roles.
  2. Human Resource Department will initiate Leadership Programs for employee development.
  3. All such programs will be for the Senior positions and for employees who manage teams to develop their Management Skills.

2.6. Training Assessment

This is important to assess the training to evaluate the impact and to plan better for future. At IPAK, we follow two levels of assessment following the Kirkpatrick Model:

  • Training Feedback
  • Learning Assessment

Training Feedback

This assessment analyses the quality of the training session conducted. This assessment is based on the following points:

  • Quality of Content
  • Relevance of the content
  • Trainer's assessment
  • Duration of training
  • Facilitation

Learning Assessment

  1. Learning Assessment is a process to evaluate the impact of the training on the employee's "Learning Curve".
  2. Where applicable, the trainer will have a Pre & Post quiz on the content of the training. The Learning Curve will be evaluated based on Pre & Post Quiz results.
  3. If any employee scores less than 70% in a post quiz, then he/she is recommended for retraining.

2.7. Skill Matrix

  1. Skill Matrix is the tool to identify the skill gap to manage the training needs of the employee.
  2. The concerned department will identify the Core Skills of every position for capability building to perform in the job role.
  3. On the defined competencies, Line Manager will assess the employee against each competence on one of the following levels:
    • No knowledge
    • Basic knowledge but no skill
    • Can practice with help
    • Can do alone
    • Can train others
  4. All such assessments will be discussed and shared with the employee.
  5. Human Resource Department will keep a track of the skill matrix and its execution for all the employees.
  6. Line Manager will visit Skill Matrix twice a year to assess the skill level and the improvement curve to plan the future development plans for the employee.
  7. The purpose of the skill matrix is to identify the skill gaps, and this is not a source for the employee's Annual Assessment. For Assessment, the basis will be the objectives assigned to the employee through Performance Management System.

2.8. Training Need Analysis

Human Resource Department will develop a Training Need Analysis based on:

  • Competence Framework
  • Skill Matrix
  • Leadership Development needs

This analysis will cover the overall organization's training needs.

2.9. Training Calendar

Based on the TNA, Human Resource Department will finalize a Training Calendar and escalate to the concerned Departments.

2.10. Budget & Approvals

  1. At the time of annual budgeting, Training budget for the next year will be approved. All the training activities will be managed within the budget.
  2. Any additional cost will be approved by Head of HR and CEO.

2.11. Process Flow

  1. Based on TNA, Human Resource Department will initiate the "Organizational Development Booklet" which will have the details of upcoming training courses and Training Calendar.
  2. Employee will raise the request for required training based on the feedback received from the Line Manager.
  3. Line Manager will validate the training request based on employee's development needs.
  4. Department Head will finalize the training participation and send the details to Human Resource Department.
  5. Human Resource Department will book the employee in the upcoming trainings available in the calendar and training enrollment confirmation will be sent to the Employee and the Line Manager.
  6. Line Manager will ensure that the employee participates in the training on the given time, date and venue.
  7. The Employee will participate in the training session.
  8. Human Resource Department will ensure the Training Assessments.
  9. Human Resource Department will furnish training assessment to the Line Manager to plan further employee's development.

Vehicle Balloting Policy

1. About Policy

1.1. Objective

This policy outlines the details for employees regarding purchase of company vehicle which the management has placed for disposal under common pool for balloting.

1.2. Scope

This policy is applicable to all the eligible employees of International Packaging Films Ltd.

1.3. Exception

All the exceptions of the policy will be approved by the Head of HR.

1.4. Frequency of Review

This policy will be reviewed on need basis if any changes are required.

1.5. Authority

The Company reserves the right to amend this policy or withdraw it in full at any time without assigning any reason.

1.6. Applicability

This policy is applicable with effect from Jan 01, 2026.

2. Policy Guidelines

2.1. Eligibility

  1. All confirmed, permanent employees of International Packaging Films Ltd. are eligible who don't have any company-maintained vehicle or provision of Vehicle Allowance as per their entitlement.
  2. Any employee can purchase one vehicle at a time: once in every five years.
  3. Employee, who already has company maintained vehicle or vehicle Allowance, neither can participate in the balloting nor can purchase any vehicle through balloting.

2.2. Entitlement

  1. Post the results of the balloting, qualifying employees will be able to purchase the vehicles at 75% of Market Value.
  2. Market Value will be assessed through Procurement Department.
  3. Employee will pay transfer of ownership cost once the balloting decisions are finalized.

2.3. Terms & Conditions

  1. The vehicle will be sold on "as is where is" basis.
  2. Only one nomination per employee will be allowed for balloting. Employees sending more than one nomination will not be included in the ballot.
  3. At all times, International Packaging Films Ltd. reserves the right to dispose of its assets in a way it deems appropriate.
  4. The employee will not sell the vehicle for a period of 12 months from the date of handing over of the vehicle. The company will retain the file of the vehicle for the period of 12 months.

2.4. Procedure

  1. HR will inform employees regarding the launch of Vehicle Balloting process through Corporate Communication along with information of available company vehicles, market value, application process and respective cut-off date of application submission.
  2. Employee will follow the stepwise process to apply and submit their application for respective vehicle as per defined process.
  3. The last date of submission/cut-off will be mentioned in the email by HR for the applicants to comply.
  4. The date of balloting will be decided by the management.
  5. The names of the qualifying employees will be communicated through Corporate Communication and company notice boards after balloting.
  6. Delivery date of vehicles will be intimated to the successful applicants by Admin Department after all the formalities mentioned above have been completed.
  7. Once the vehicle has been handed over to the successful employee, insurance will stand to cease.
  8. Employee shall not claim any maintenance after receiving the vehicle as the vehicle is sold on "as is where is" basis.
  9. The employee will transfer the vehicle in his/her own name within 15 days from the date of the handing over of the vehicle.
  10. The employee will submit the proof of the transfer of the vehicle to the company within 15 days from the date of the handing over of the vehicle.

2.5. Payment

  1. Qualifying employees can make payments in the favor of International Packaging Films Ltd. using any of the following methods:
    • Demand Draft
    • Pay Order
    • Cheque
  2. Employees will be required to submit the payment to Admin Department to obtain the vehicle within 10 working days of balloting announcement otherwise they will have to forego their right to the vehicle.
